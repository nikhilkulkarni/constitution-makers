Constituent Assembly of India

The Constitution of India was written by a Constituent Assembly comprising of 389 members elected from various parts of India. These members represented diverse regions, political parties, and communities across the country.

Search any location in the textbox on the right to know who represented the location in the Constituent Assembly.

Significant Contributors

B.R. Ambedkar: He was the Chairman of the Drafting Committee and is often referred to as the principal architect of the Constitution. Ambedkar played a crucial role in shaping the document to ensure it enshrined principles of justice, equality, and liberty.

B.N. Rau: Served as the constitutional advisor and prepared the preliminary draft of the Constitution. His work laid the foundation for the final document.

Surendra Nath Mukherjee: Acted as the chief draftsman, helping to put the Constitution into legislative form.

Prem Behari Narain Raizada: He was the calligrapher who handwrote the original Constitution in both English and Hindi, using a flowing italic style.


Other key figures included leaders like Jawaharlal Nehru, Sardar Vallabhbhai Patel, and Maulana Abul Kalam Azad. The assembly's composition aimed to reflect the pluralistic society of India, incorporating voices from various backgrounds.
